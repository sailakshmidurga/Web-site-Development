Temples
Meenakshi-Sundareswarar Temple, Madurai

It is dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi (a form of Parvati) and her consort Lord Sundareswarar (a form of Shiva). The temple is a significant symbol of Dravidian architecture and a major center of Shaivism and Shaktism.

Kamakshi Amman Temple, Kanchipuram

The Kamakshi Amman Temple in Kanchipuram, Tamil Nadu, is one of the most sacred shrines dedicated to Goddess Shakti (Parvati). The name Kamakshi is derived from “Ka” (Saraswati - knowledge), “Ma” (Lakshmi - wealth), and “Akshi” (eyes) — meaning "She who has the eyes of Lakshmi and Saraswati." This temple is one of the 51 Shakti Peethas, making it an important destination for devotees of Shaktism.

Ramanathaswamy-Temple, Rameshwaram

The Ramanathaswamy Temple in Rameshwaram, Tamil Nadu, is one of the most revered pilgrimage sites in India, sacred to Lord Shiva. It is one of the twelve Jyotirlinga temples and holds immense significance in Shaivism as well as Vaishnavism due to its association with Lord Rama.

Brihadeeswarar Temple, Thanjavur

Built by Raja Raja Chola in the 11th century, it's a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The temple has a massive Shiva lingam and a 216-foot vimana (tower). It showcases Chola architectural brilliance.

Dedicated to Lord Shiva in the form of Agni (fire). It’s one of the Pancha Bhoota Sthalams and located near the Arunachala hill. The temple is central to the Karthigai Deepam festival.

Kapaleeshwarar Temple, Chennai

Dedicated to Goddess Kali, built by the Chola dynasty. It is said to commemorate the cosmic dance competition between Shiva and Kali. A serene and powerful Shakti peeth in the heart of Chidambaram.
